# Business Overview — Zühlke Managed Services

## What We Do

Zühlke Managed Services (MS) is a business unit within the Zühlke Group that provides ongoing, outcome-based service delivery for clients' strategic and critical digital assets. We keep applications running, stable, and improving after go-live.

This is a fundamentally different model from Zühlke's core project delivery business. In project delivery, Zühlke sells engineering effort toward a defined goal. In Managed Services, Zühlke sells a recurring service with guaranteed outcomes — the client pays a subscription fee in exchange for defined service levels.

## Mission

> "We provide premium service delivery for our clients' strategic and critical digital assets, sourcing our expertise from Zühlke's deep engineering-led heritage and long tradition of trust. Our mission is to ensure the continuous operation and proactive improvement of digital products by shifting from selling effort to delivering guaranteed service outcomes."

## Strategic Context

Recurring revenue through Managed Services is a key driver of Zühlke Group's **"Innovate, Build, and Operate" strategy (2025–2027)**. The shift from effort-based to outcome-based delivery:

- Provides clients with predictable costs and peace of mind
- Builds deep, accountability-based client relationships
- Drives recurring revenue and sustainable growth for Zühlke
- Leverages economies of scale through long-term contracts

Zühlke brings over five decades of consulting and engineering excellence. MS extends that relationship beyond project completion, managing clients' solutions throughout their entire product lifecycle.

## Service Model: Protect → Evolve → Transform

The MS service model operates on three levels:
- **Protect**: keep the application stable, secure, and running within SLAs
- **Evolve**: proactively improve the service over time
- **Transform**: use AI-enhanced operations and modern practices to act as a proactive utility provider, not just a maintenance team

## Core Services

### Application Management Services (AMS)
Ongoing support and maintenance for complex applications after go-live:
- Daily monitoring and maintenance
- Update management
- Issue resolution and prevention
- Performance optimization
- Incident management within agreed SLAs

AMS takes ownership when development peaks and the product moves toward live. It covers the full product lifecycle from Beta through Live and into Retirement.

### Cloud Managed Services (CMS)
Cloud operations support throughout the product lifecycle:
- Starts early (Discovery/Alpha phase) and runs continuously
- Cloud infrastructure monitoring, management, and optimization
- Enables clients to leverage cloud benefits without navigating its complexity

## Pricing Model

MS engagements use a **subscription-based model** — recurring monthly or quarterly fees in exchange for defined service levels. This is not T&M (time and materials) or fixed-price project work.

Key commercial principles:
- Zühlke owns the service outcome, not just the capacity
- Team staffing is Zühlke's responsibility — MS is not staff augmentation
- SLA commitments are contractual, not best-effort
- Pricing is driven by business criticality, SLA tier, support hours, and system complexity

## Geographic Focus

Primarily the DACH region (Germany, Austria, Switzerland). German-language requirements are common for client-facing deliverables and contracts. Zühlke also operates in other markets (UK, Singapore) with region-specific contract addenda.

## Certifications

- **ISO 20000**: International standard for IT Service Management. Zühlke MS is certified, meaning all service management processes (incident handling, change management, service requests, continuous improvement) are independently audited. This is a commercial differentiator, especially with clients in regulated industries.
- **ISO 27001**: All MS projects must comply with ISO 27001 (Information Security Management). Per-project classification and documentation requirements apply.

## Key Differentiation

- Engineering-led heritage — same standards used in initial development, applied to ongoing operations
- ISO 20000 certification — clients in banking, healthcare, and public sector often require certified providers
- AI-enhanced operations — proactive, not reactive
- Single point of accountability per engagement (Service Manager)
- Deep long-term client relationships that evolve into strategic partnerships
