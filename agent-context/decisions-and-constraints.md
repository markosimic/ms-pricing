# Decisions and Constraints — Zühlke Managed Services

These are known rules, constraints, and past decisions that govern how MS operates. They exist for specific reasons and should not be changed without understanding the rationale.

---

## Commercial & Contractual Constraints

### Always use approved contract templates. Never adapt from previous deals.
Templates are versioned and reviewed quarterly. Located in SharePoint > MS Contracts & Templates. They are legally reviewed and approved. Using old or custom contracts introduces legal risk and creates inconsistency.

### DACH contracts must stay in German. Do not translate the template.
Contracts for CH/DE/AT clients are in German by legal requirement and client expectation. If a client challenges the language or structure, escalate to MS Commercial Lead. Do not negotiate contract terms directly without them in the loop.

### Gate 0/1 is mandatory. No contracting without all gates cleared.
This prevents deals moving forward without the right commercials, SLAs, and cost plan in place. Skipping these gates leads to rework and delivery problems. If a deal feels pre-sold or complex, stop and escalate before proceeding.

### Never accept penalty-only clauses. Always counter with bonus/malus.
Zühlke must have upside as well as downside tied to SLA performance. Penalty-only structures are commercially unfair and create one-sided risk.

### Budget warranty (Gewährleistung) into the base fee at ~2–4% of initial project investment.
Warranty is a legal obligation. It must be priced in, not absorbed post-contract. Do not guarantee warranty for third-party solutions.

### Do not cross-reference project contracts in maintenance contracts.
Project contracts expire before maintenance contracts do. Cross-referencing creates legal gaps and confusion.

### Subcontractor SLAs must be aligned with client SLAs.
If external partners are involved, their commitments must match Zühlke's commitments to the client. Misaligned subcontractor SLAs create delivery risk that Zühlke absorbs.

### Move client obligation lists to an annex, not the main contract body.
Long obligation lists in the main body overwhelm stakeholders and slow sign-off. Keep the contract body clean.

## Service Delivery Constraints

### Zühlke manages the service. We do not deliver staff.
The contract must state that team staffing is Zühlke's responsibility. MS is not staff augmentation. This is a fundamental model distinction — it affects how contracts are written and how the service is positioned.

### Stick to standard SLA packages. No custom combinations without strong reason.
Custom SLA tiers (non-standard availability hours, holiday exceptions, etc.) create delivery and pricing complexity. If a client requests custom combinations, the next higher standard tier applies. Custom exceptions require documented justification.

### ISO 20000 processes are mandatory. You cannot bypass them.
Zühlke MS is certified. Bypassing the service desk, skipping the change process, or improvising incident classification are not options. If a client asks for a custom process that conflicts with ISO 20000, that is a scope and risk conversation — not a "yes, no problem."

### Business criticality must be assessed honestly, not inflated or deflated.
Over-classification inflates cost and creates unsustainable support commitments. Under-classification means slower response times than the business can tolerate — that becomes a relationship problem. The assessment should be evidence-based and agreed by both sides.

### The service catalogue must be compared against the final contract at onboarding start.
In past engagements, offers were adapted during negotiations without updating the service catalogue. This caused service and budget surprises during delivery. Always check for discrepancies before the maintenance phase begins.

## Language & Communication Constraints

### Define language expectations during the RFP/SoW phase, not during delivery.
For DACH clients, German is expected at most client-facing touchpoints. Resistance to non-German-speaking staff is a known pattern. Surfacing this late creates conflict.

### Use the standard Language Plan template for mixed-language teams.
Document explicitly which roles must be German-speaking and where English is acceptable. Use the prepared communication script when explaining mixed-language teams to clients — do not improvise this conversation.

## ISO 27001 Constraints

### Every MS project must be ISO 27001 compliant.
Security classification and documentation are mandatory per project. The Engagement Manager decides the final classification level. Classification must be reported in Vertec.

## Operational Known Issues & Past Decisions

### "Hypercare" and "Warranty" are different things.
This distinction has caused confusion in the past. Warranty is a legal obligation (liability for defects). Hypercare is a service option (elevated post-go-live support). They are priced and positioned differently.

### The client will try to pass failure costs to Zühlke in the contract.
This is an expected negotiation behaviour. When a managed system fails, the client loses money and will try to make Zühlke contractually liable. Document your risk assessment before negotiations start so you know what you can and cannot accept.

### Custom reports for clients create significant overhead.
Standard monthly or quarterly reports should be the default. Custom reports are a cost driver — verify the client is willing to pay for them before agreeing.

### Language Plan is required before deal start, not after.
Discovered through delivery experience: if language expectations aren't documented upfront, they surface as conflicts during engagement. Standardised response scripting now exists for this scenario.

### MS Enablement is required before handling a first deal.
New team members must complete the 90-minute MS Enablement session before their first deal. This was standardised because people from project delivery backgrounds misapply project delivery assumptions to MS deals, leading to pricing and structuring errors.
